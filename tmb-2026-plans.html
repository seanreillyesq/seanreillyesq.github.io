The whole trip on one page: Sean and Alex walk the full 166 km loop over nine days while Nicole leapfrogs the campervan between valley bases and shuttles the walkers to and from the trailheads. Walking maps are on terrain; driving maps on satellite.

Emergency numbers. 112 works in all three countries. France: PGHM Chamonix mountain rescue +33 4 50 53 16 89. Italy: 112 (single number, covers mountain rescue). Switzerland: 144 (rescue) / 1414 (Rega helicopter). Sean's mobile: +44 7957 247666. If separated with no signal: carry on to that day's van base - they are listed on every day card below.

The numbers. Walking: 166 km over 9 days (longest day 25.4 km, D4). Driving: about 310 km of relocations plus shuttle runs at Val Veny and La Fouly - roughly 420 km all in. Direction: anti-clockwise from Les Houches. Dates: arrive Tue 28 July, Day 1 on Thu 30 July, finish Fri 7 August, depart Sat 8 August.

The rules that bite. Bellevue: gate locks at 19:30, arrivals from 16:00, passports for all three at check-in, hard-standing pitches, no water or waste-dump on site (free borne at Carrefour Market, Chamonix entrance). Le Pontet: €22.18 balance due on arrival. Italian Val Ferret road: cars only as far as Lavachey, 260 paid spaces, 09:00-19:00. Switzerland: motorway vignette (~CHF 40) before Grand St Bernard; buy groceries in Italy (Swiss prices roughly double). Argentière: no bookings - Nicole arrives at 14:00 sharp when reception opens.
