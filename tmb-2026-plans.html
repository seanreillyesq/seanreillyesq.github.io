Nine days walking round Mont Blanc, eleven nights in a van. Sean and Alex walk the full 166 km loop; Nicole leapfrogs the van between valley campsites and shuttles the walkers to and from the trailheads. This page is the whole plan - maps, bookings, phone numbers, and what to do when it goes sideways.

If it all goes wrong. 112 gets mountain rescue in all three countries. France also has PGHM Chamonix direct: +33 4 50 53 16 89. Switzerland: 144, or 1414 for the Rega helicopter. Sean's mobile: +44 7957 247666. Separated with no signal? Don't wander - carry on to that day's van base. It's on every day card below.

The rules that bite. Bellevue's gate locks at 19:30 and that's that - arrivals 16:00-19:30, passports for all three at check-in, pitches are hard standing (tent pegs won't go in), and there's no water or waste dump on site; the free borne is at the Carrefour Market as you come into Chamonix. Le Pontet wants the €22.18 balance on arrival. The Italian Val Ferret road only lets cars as far as Lavachey - 260 paid spaces, 09:00-19:00, and they close the road when they're gone. Switzerland: buy the CHF 40 vignette before the Grand St Bernard motorway, and do the big shop in Italy first - Swiss supermarket prices are roughly double. Argentière takes no bookings at all; the job there is simply to be at reception at 14:00 when it opens.
